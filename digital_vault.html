Privacy Policy for Digital Legacy Vault

Last Updated: November 15, 2025

Introduction

Welcome to Digital Legacy Vault ("the App"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your data with transparency. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and why we require certain device permissions to provide our services. Our core principle is data minimization; we only ask for what is absolutely necessary for the App to function securely.

Our Core Privacy Principle: Zero Knowledge

Your financial data, personal documents, notes, and secrets (your "Vault Data") are encrypted on your device using a master key that only you control. Your Vault Data is never uploaded to our servers, and we, the developers, have zero access to it.

Required Android Permissions and Justifications

Our App requests the following permissions to enable specific features. The App cannot function correctly without them.

1. Camera Permission (android.permission.CAMERA)

  • Why we need it: The Camera permission is required for our "Secure Transfer" feature. This feature allows you to migrate your encrypted Vault from your old phone to a new one easily and securely.
  • How it's used: When you select the "Import Key" option on your new device, the App uses the camera to scan a QR code displayed on your old device. This QR code contains your unique master encryption key. The camera is only active when you explicitly initiate this scanning process.
  • What we DON'T do: We do not record, store, or transmit any images or videos from your camera. The camera's use is temporary and solely for reading the transfer QR code.

2. Biometric Permission (android.permission.USE_BIOMETRIC)

  • Why we need it: We use this permission to provide a convenient and highly secure way for you to unlock your Vault using your device's built-in biometrics (fingerprint or face recognition).
  • How it's used: When you enable biometric login, our App leverages Android's native biometric framework. The App simply asks the Android system, "Is this the valid device owner?". The system handles the verification securely on-device.
  • What we DON'T do: Your biometric data (fingerprint or face data) is never accessed, read, stored, or transmitted by our App. All biometric processing is handled securely by your device's operating system.

3. Post Notifications Permission (android.permission.POST_NOTIFICATIONS)

  • Why we need it: This permission is critical for the App's primary "dead man's switch" functionality. The App's purpose is to alert your emergency contact if you fail to check in within your specified validation period.
  • How it's used: Our App uses background processes to monitor your validation schedule. If your check-in deadline is approaching, the App must send you a local notification to remind you. Without this permission, you would not receive these crucial reminders, which could lead to a false emergency alert being sent.
  • What we DON'T do: We do not use notifications for marketing or promotional purposes. All notifications are strictly related to the core security and validation functions of the App.

4. Background Execution & Start on Boot

  • Why we need it: To ensure the reliability of our validation reminders, our app must be able to run periodic checks in the background, even if the App is closed or the device has been restarted.
  • How it's used: This is directly tied to the Notifications feature. A lightweight background process runs occasionally to check if your validation deadline is near. This ensures that reminder notifications can be scheduled reliably.
  • What we DON'T do: The background process is optimized for minimal battery impact. It does not access personal data; it only checks the last validation timestamp stored securely within the App's private storage.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.
